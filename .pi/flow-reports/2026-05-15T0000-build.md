## Summary

Fixed flaky illuminate-mode tests in `scramble.test.ts`. Root cause: `randomizedCenter()` could place the ripple origin on a space character in the test string `'Hello world!'`. At 30ms `initialTimeOffset` the radius was <1, so adjacent non-space characters fell outside the depth band and no ANSI was emitted, causing intermittent failures. Changed test text to `'Helloworld!!'` (no spaces) and tightened TPS KPI render timing from 100ms to 40ms post-flash to avoid afterglow-only windows where `dur < 210` suppresses echo pops. Full suite now passes consistently (5/5 scramble-only runs + full 977-test run).

## Reasoning

1. `randomizedCenter` picks a position in the 20%–80% range of text length. For a 12-character string, position 5 is the space between "Hello" and "world".
2. When the center lands on a space, neighbors at distance 1 have negative depth at 30ms because the radius is <1, so no characters are scrambled.
3. Removing spaces guarantees the center character is always a scramblable character, eliminating the zero-ANSI window.
4. The TPS KPI timing fragility was caused by a 100ms post-flash window occasionally landing in an afterglow-only state where `dur < 210` suppresses echo pops. Reducing to 40ms avoids this window.
